Rock art at risk

Across Australia there are more than 100,000 rock-art sites, with millions of unique indigenous works, created over tens of thousands of years. Now mining exploration is threatening significant sites.

About this map

What the map shows

We have used data from each state and territory as well as national mining data. Due to the differing terminology between states we have limited what we display to similar data sets. Map data was downloaded between February 22 and March 5, 2013, so our map is a snapshot of mines and mining activities for that period.

  1. Rock-Art Regions: Significant rock-art regions according to Professor Paul Taçon, Chair in Rock Art Research, Griffith University. Overlapping regions indicate multiple rock-art styles.
  2. Mines: Includes all operating mines and major resource projects. State and territory data was used where available; otherwise national data was used.
  3. Pipelines: Includes petroleum, liquids, and gas pipelines based on available state and territory data.
  4. Leases and Licences: Includes current exploration licences, tenements, claims, leases and licences based on available state and territory data. We have excluded anything labelled as 'application' or where relevant fields are 'null'.
